Welcome to BS Farm

BS Farm is a dynamic agricultural enterprise located in Jenoi Village, Rural Gambia. Owned and managed by Mr. Buba Saidy, our business focuses on poultry production (broilers and layers) and small‑scale horticulture, with plans to expand into a medium‑sized vegetable farm specialising in okra and cucumber. We are dedicated to supplying fresh, healthy, and affordable produce to our local community and beyond.

With a clear vision for growth and sustainability, BS Farm is committed to contributing to food security, rural employment, and economic development in The Gambia. Whether you are a customer seeking high‑quality chicken, eggs, or vegetables, or a partner interested in agricultural innovation, we invite you to join us on this journey.

About Us / Business Description

BS Farm was established by Mr. Buba Saidy, a passionate farmer with deep roots in the Jenoi community. The business operates as a sole proprietorship and is set to be formally registered soon. Our core activities include:

  • Poultry: Broiler production (500 birds per cycle, 3 cycles annually) and layer hens (200 birds) for fresh eggs.
  • Horticulture: Cultivation of okra and cucumber on a 0.5‑hectare plot, with plans to expand and adopt irrigation.

Our mission is to produce and supply fresh, healthy, and affordable agricultural products while empowering our community through employment and sustainable practices. We envision BS Farm becoming a model agribusiness in The Gambia, recognised for efficiency, quality, and profitability.

Key milestones:

  • 2026: Launch of poultry and vegetable operations
  • 2027: Expansion to 1,000 birds per cycle and 2‑3 hectares of vegetables
  • 2028: Formal business registration and value‑added products

The Challenge / Problem We Solve

Agriculture in The Gambia faces persistent challenges that limit productivity and profitability:

  • Unreliable water supply: Rain‑fed farming is vulnerable to climate variability, affecting both poultry and vegetable production.
  • High production costs: Feed, medicine, and irrigation inputs are expensive, squeezing smallholder margins.
  • Limited market access: Many farmers struggle to connect with consistent buyers, leading to post‑harvest losses.
  • Low adoption of modern techniques: Without access to training and technology, yields remain suboptimal.

BS Farm directly addresses these issues by using efficient production methods, diversifying income streams, and building strong local market links – ensuring that our community has reliable access to fresh, high‑quality food.

Our Approach / What Makes Us Different

BS Farm combines proven agricultural practices with a commitment to innovation and sustainability:

  • Integrated production: Poultry manure fertilises vegetable plots, reducing waste and input costs.
  • Planned growth: We start small and scale systematically, reinvesting profits to expand infrastructure and capacity.
  • Local sourcing: We prioritise local suppliers for feed and other inputs, strengthening the local economy.
  • Water security: Our future investment in solar‑powered irrigation will ensure year‑round vegetable production, even in dry seasons.
  • Quality assurance: Strict vaccination and hygiene protocols ensure healthy birds and safe produce.

By focusing on efficiency, quality, and community, we are building a resilient agribusiness that can adapt to challenges and seize new opportunities.

Market Opportunity

The Gambian market for poultry and vegetables is robust and growing:

  • High demand: Chicken and eggs are dietary staples, and vegetables like okra and cucumber are essential in local cuisine.
  • Underserved rural areas: Many rural communities lack consistent access to fresh, affordable produce – a gap BS Farm can fill.
  • Tourism and hospitality: Hotels and restaurants in urban centres require reliable supplies of quality ingredients.
  • Regional potential: With improved production, we can supply neighbouring countries facing similar challenges.

BS Farm is positioned to capture these opportunities through direct sales to households, B2B agreements with local businesses, and a strong reputation for freshness and reliability.

Operations & Business Model

BS Farm runs a diversified operation with clear revenue streams:

  • Poultry: Broilers sold as live birds or dressed chicken; layers provide a constant supply of fresh eggs.
  • Vegetables: Okra and cucumber harvested and sold directly to consumers, local shops, and restaurants.

Key operational details:

  • Broiler cycles: 500 birds × 3 cycles per year
  • Layer flock: 200 hens producing eggs daily
  • Vegetable plot: 0.5 hectares (expanding to 2‑3 ha with irrigation)
  • Workforce: Owner (Mr. Buba Saidy), family members, and 1‑2 hired workers

We are currently investing in infrastructure, including poultry housing and irrigation equipment, to boost productivity and prepare for future growth.

Our Team & Impact

Mr. Buba Saidy is the owner and manager of BS Farm. With years of experience in agriculture and a passion for rural development, he leads day‑to‑day operations and strategic planning. He is supported by a dedicated team of family members and local workers, all of whom receive training and fair wages.

Our impact:

  • Economic: Creating jobs and generating income for the community.
  • Social: Improving access to nutritious food and supporting local food security.
  • Environmental: Promoting sustainable practices and efficient resource use.

We believe that a thriving farm benefits everyone – from the workers who tend the crops to the families who enjoy our products.

  • Vegetable yield: 1+ ton per season (expanding)
  • Jobs created: 3‑5 direct positions
  • Community reach: Serving 100+ households weekly
