🌱 Welcome to BS Farm

BS Farm is a dynamic agricultural enterprise in Jenoi Village, Rural Gambia, owned by Mr. Buba Saidy. We specialise in poultry (broilers & layers) and horticulture (okra & cucumber), with a clear path to becoming a medium‑scale, diversified agribusiness.

We are driven by a mission to deliver fresh, healthy, and affordable produce while creating jobs and strengthening food security in our community. Whether you are a customer, partner, or investor, we invite you to grow with us.

📖 About Us

BS Farm was founded by Mr. Buba Saidy, a farmer with deep roots in Jenoi. The business currently operates as a sole proprietorship and will be formally registered soon. Our core activities:

  • Poultry: 500 broilers per cycle (3 cycles/year) + 200 layers for eggs.
  • Horticulture: 0.5 ha of okra and cucumber, expanding with irrigation.

Mission: Produce fresh, affordable food while empowering our community through sustainable practices and fair employment.

Vision: Become a model agribusiness in The Gambia – efficient, profitable, and community‑centred.

Key milestones: Launch (2026) → Expand to 1,000 birds & 2‑3 ha (2027) → Formal registration & value‑added products (2028).

⚠️ The Challenge

Gambian agriculture faces persistent hurdles:

  • Unreliable water supply – climate‑dependent rains affect both poultry and crops.
  • High input costs – feed, medicine, and irrigation strain smallholder margins.
  • Limited market access – many farmers struggle to find consistent buyers.
  • Low tech adoption – lack of training and modern tools keeps yields low.

BS Farm tackles these head‑on with efficient production, diversified income, and strong local market links – ensuring reliable access to quality food for our community.

💡 Our Approach

  • Integrated production – poultry manure fertilises vegetable plots, cutting waste and costs.
  • Planned scaling – reinvest profits to expand infrastructure systematically.
  • Local sourcing – prioritise Gambian suppliers for feed and inputs.
  • Water security – future solar‑powered irrigation ensures year‑round vegetable production.
  • Quality assurance – strict vaccination and biosecurity for healthy birds and safe produce.

This approach builds a resilient, adaptable agribusiness that seizes new opportunities while serving the community.

📊 Market Opportunity

The demand for poultry and vegetables in The Gambia is strong and growing:

  • High staple demand – chicken, eggs, okra, and cucumber are everyday essentials.
  • Rural supply gap – many villages lack consistent access to fresh produce.
  • Tourism & hospitality – hotels and restaurants need reliable quality supplies.
  • Regional export potential – with increased production, we can serve neighbouring countries.

BS Farm is well‑placed to capture these channels through direct sales, B2B agreements, and a reputation for freshness and reliability.

⚙️ Operations & Business Model

  • Poultry: Broilers sold live or dressed; layers supply fresh eggs daily.
  • Vegetables: Okra and cucumber harvested and sold to consumers, shops, and restaurants.

Key figures:

  • Broilers: 500 birds × 3 cycles/year
  • Layers: 200 hens (eggs daily)
  • Vegetable plot: 0.5 ha (expanding to 2‑3 ha with irrigation)

We are investing in poultry housing and irrigation equipment to boost productivity and prepare for future growth.
